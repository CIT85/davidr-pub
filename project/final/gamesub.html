Introduction

Video game graphics have evolved drastically over the decades. They have gone from pixelated graphics to hyper-realistic 3D environments.

The Beginning

1970s - The Beginning of Gaming

In the 1970s, video games were just beginning to emerge. The graphics consisted of simple shapes and limited color palette.

1980s - 8 and 16 bit Graphics

The 1980s saw a significant improvement in graphics thanks in part to the rise of inhome consoles. Games introduced more detailed sprites and a wider color palette.

Rise of 3D Graphics

The 1990s saw a huge breakthrough with the introduction of 3D graphics.

2000s - Imporvement of 3D Graphics

The 2000s saw an improvement in realistic textures, improved lighting, and detailed environments.

Modern Era

2010s - Age of HD and 4K

In the 2010s, HD and 4K resolutions became standard. This offered detail and clarity never seen before.

2020s - The Future of Graphics

The 2020s seeks to advance graphics using raytracing and pathtracing, which creates more natural lighting.
