Video Games and Learning

Video games are being increasingly integrated into school curriculums to promote engagement and interactive learning. Games like Minecraft: Education Edition are used in classrooms to teach subjects including mathematics, science, and history, allowing students to explore and build virtual worlds while learning fundamental concepts.

The First Easter Egg

The first known "Easter egg" in a video game was hidden in the Atari game "Adventure" released in 1980. It contained a secret room where the creator's name, Warren Robinett, was displayed.

The Most Expensive Video Game Every Develeoped

The most expensive video game ever developed as of 2024 is "Grand Theft Auto V," which cost approximately $265 million to produce and market.

Gaming as Therapy

Video games are increasingly used in therapeutic settings, helping patients with PTSD, anxiety, and depression manage symptoms through controlled exposure and achievement-based tasks.
